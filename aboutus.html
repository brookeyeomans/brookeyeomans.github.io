Paella Preservation Society

Welcome to La Paella Co., a proud chapter of the Paella Preservation Society. Our mission is to share the art of
traditional paella preparation with the world. We believe that everyone, no matter where they are, should have the
opportunity to savor the authentic flavors of Spain. Our site is a treasure trove of knowledge and resources, offering
a comprehensive guide to mastering the various methods and tools required to prepare this cultural dish with ease.
Join us in celebrating the rich heritage of paella and embark on a culinary journey that transcends borders and brings
the essence of Spain to your kitchen.

Humble Beginnings

Valencia in Eastern Spain is the undisputed home of paella. It is one of the largest natural ports in the
Mediterranean and has been one of the most important rice-producing areas in Spain since rice was
introduced by the Moors over 1200 years ago. Paella was also traditionally eaten with a group of people
straight from the pan in which it was cooked with each person using his own wooden spoon.

By the Fire

Paella was originally farmers’ and farm labourers’ food, cooked by the workers over a wooden fire for the
lunchtime meal. It was made with rice, plus whatever was on hand around the rice fields and countryside:
tomatoes, onions, and snails, with a few beans added for flavor and texture. Rabbit or duck might also
have been added, and for special occasions, chicken plus a touch of saffron for
an extra special color and flavor.
